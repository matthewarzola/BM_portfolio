## 1. Portfolio Definition
**Who the website is for:**  
Potential employers, recruiters, and hiring managers who want to evaluate my UI/UX engineering and front-end development skills.

**What the website is for:**  
To showcase my design-to-code skills, highlight selected projects, present my professional experience, and provide a clear point of contact for opportunities.

**Target Audience:**  
- HR and recruiters from tech companies  
- UI/UX team leads and front-end engineering managers  
- Startup founders looking for design-focused developers  
- Clients who need UI/UX or front-end work

**Website Personality:**  
Modern, clean, professional, with a subtle creative flair. Tone should feel approachable and confident.

## 2. Page Sections and Content

### **Hero / Introduction**
- Short headline: 
    - "Hi, I'm Benedict — a UI/UX Software Engineer with a growing passion for Cybersecurity."
- Subtext: 1–2 sentences describing skill focus
    - With a strong foundation in UI/UX engineering, I’m now expanding into cybersecurity through continuous learning, labs, and real-world security practices.
- Optional photo or avatar

- Call-to-action buttons: "Resume" and "Contact Me(eto mag direct sa contact section)"


### **About Me**
- Short background story (2–3 paragraphs) about experience in UI/UX engineering
- Skills list (HTML, CSS, SCSS, UIkit, Vue, JavaScript, Git, UX principles)
- Tools used (Figma, VS Code, GitHub, etc.)
- Personal values: clean design, accessibility, consistency

### **Experience & Education**
- List of job roles and responsibilities
- Key achievements and contributions
- Education background
- Certifications (Google Cybersecurity course, etc.)

### **Projects / Portfolio Gallery**
For each project include:
- Project title
- Thumbnail mockup or screenshot
- Short description of the challenge and solution
- Role and responsibilities
- Tools/technologies used
- Optional: links to GitHub or live demo

### **Case Studies (Optional)**
- Deep-dive breakdown of 1–3 projects
- Problem → Process → Solution → Result
- Wireframes, flows, or component systems

### **Testimonials (Optional)**
- Short quotes from teammates, mentors, or clients

### **Contact**
- Email  
- LinkedIn link  
- GitHub link  
- Optional contact form
